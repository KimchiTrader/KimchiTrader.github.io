Financial Analysis

Fundamental analysis uses macro and microeconomic factors to identify the intrinsic value of a stock. The goal is to purchase undervalued stocks and sell overvalued stocks.

Quantitative analysis uses mathematical techniques to identify optimal market conditions for purchasing opportunities.

Kimchi Trader combines both of these approaches, using quantitative algorithms to analyze public financial statements.

Data Availability

All companies in the United States are required to fill out a comprehensive report of financial performance to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC). These reports consist of four primary financial statements and must be completed prior to the end of each financial quarter.

Each statement includes business metrics like revenues, cash flows, debt levels, and more.

Fundamental analysis can further our understanding of these reports by calculating ratios to value the company. The P/E ratio, for example, measures a company's current share price relative to its per-share earnings. Kimchi Trader calculates 250+ fundamental ratios.

Artificial Intelligence

Kimchi Trader employs machine learning, a field of artificial intelligence for decision making.

Imagine a graph that plots all companies as individual data points. It has financial ratios like the P/E ratio and operating cash flow on one axis, and yearly earnings on another. In theory, a 3D plane maps the minimum distance between data points, helping our algorithm predict which financial data points are likely to indicate profitable stock picks.

Market Optimization

There are 11 sectors of the economy, and the results from fundamental analysis are best interpreted between companies of the same sector.

For this reason, Kimchi Trader creates 11 separate AI models that constantly adapt to new conditions within each sector.

We've simulated our algorithm on the last 5 years of market performance. Our algorithm is consistently twice as efficient as the S&P500 in each market sector.
